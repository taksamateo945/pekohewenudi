    Basketball Quotes from Legends: Words of Wisdom from NBA Icons

    Basketball is more than just a sport.

    It’s a passion, a lifestyle, a way of expressing yourself.

    Whether you play it, watch it, or coach it, basketball can teach you valuable lessons about teamwork, discipline, perseverance, and leadership.

    In this article, we have collected some of the most inspiring and motivational basketball quotes from famous players, coaches, and commentators.

    Inspirational Basketball Quotes

    Here are the best inspiring basketball quotes from some famous basketball players.

    •  “Talent wins games, but teamwork and intelligence wins championships.” ― Michael Jordan, ‘I Can’t Accept Not Trying: Michael Jordan On The Pursuit Of Excellence’.
    •  “A team will always appreciate a great individual if he’s willing to sacrifice for the group.” ―  Kareem Abdul Jabbar.
    •  “The important thing is that your teammates have to know you’re pulling for them and you really want them to be successful.” ―  Kobe Bryant.
    •  “Ask not what your teammates can do for you. Ask what you can do for them.” ―  Magic Johnson.
    •  “You can’t get much done in life if you only work on the days when you feel good.” ―  Jerry West.
    •  “Ask me to steal, block out, sacrifice, lead, dominate, anything. But it’s not what you ask of me it’s what I ask of myself.” ―  LeBron James.
    •  “A strong leader accepts blame and gives the credit. A weak leader gives blame and accepts the credit.” ― John Wooden, ‘Wooden On Leadership: How To Create A Winning Organization’.
    •  “Success is a result of consistent practice of winning skills and actions. There is nothing miraculous about the process. There is no luck involved.” ―  Bill Russell.
    •  “The game has its ups and downs, but you can never lose focus of your individual goals and you can’t let yourself be beat because of lack of effort.” ―  Michael Jordan.
    •  “The important thing is that your teammates have to know you’re pulling for them and you really want them to be successful.” ―  Kobe Bryant.
